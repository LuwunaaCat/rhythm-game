The format for charts (in this game) is:
start of repeat (beat #)
end of repeat (beat #)
tempo (bpm)
time signature

beat #, lane

A couple of notes:
1. each song will have 2 measure of count in
    (ie on a hi-hat 1 3, 1 2 3 4, then the song starts)
    so notes should start on beat 9
2. when I say "beat #", I am NOT refering to measure number
    for instance, the first beat of measure 2 will be 5, not 2
3. "time signature" means only the top number/how many beats per measure
    (ie 4/4 = 4, 6/8 = 6, etc)
4. lane referes to the key to press for the note, (D, F, J, or K)

for example:
1.0, D
2.0, F
3.0, J
4.0, K

will have notes in:
Lane 1 (D) on beat 1
Lane 2 (F) on beat 2
Lane 3 (J) on beat 3
Lane 4 (k) on beat 4

decimals are also accepted in this format:
1.0, F
1.25, J
1.5, F
1.75, J

will have notes in:
Lane 2 (F) on the "Ta" of beat 1
Lane 3 (J) on the "Ka" of beat 1
Lane 2 (F) on the "Di" of beat 1
Lane 3 (J) on the "Mi" of beat 1

eith note triplets might look like:
1.0, D
2.0, D
3.0, F
3.333, J
3.667, F
4.0, J

which will turn out like
Ta, Ta, Ta Ki Da, Ta
